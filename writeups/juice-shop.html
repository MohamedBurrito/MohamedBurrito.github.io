Project · Web Pentesting

OWASP Juice Shop Pentesting

OWASP Top 10 assessment
Burp Suite OWASP Top 10 CVSS Scoring
Recon → Exploitation → Impact Assessment → Reporting
01

Problem

OWASP Juice Shop is an intentionally vulnerable web app covering the OWASP Top 10. The goal was to treat it as a real assessment target rather than a scavenger hunt — producing findings the way a client engagement would expect.

Why this matteredJuice Shop is popular enough that solutions are easy to find online — doing it as a proper assessment with real reporting, instead of just collecting flags, is what makes it useful practice.
02

Design

Testing was done with Burp Suite as the primary interception and manipulation tool, working through the application's flows systematically rather than jumping straight to known Juice Shop challenge hints.

Why this matteredWorking through the app's actual user flows (registration, checkout, admin panel) surfaces vulnerabilities in the order a real attacker would find them, which is better practice than targeting known challenge IDs directly.
03

Implementation

Multiple vulnerability classes from the OWASP Top 10 were identified and exploited across the application, each investigated for root cause rather than just triggering the vulnerable state.

Why this matteredUnderstanding the root cause of each finding — not just the trigger — is what makes the resulting report's mitigation advice actually specific and useful rather than generic boilerplate.
04

Result

Findings were compiled into CVSS-scored reports with mitigation strategies for each vulnerability, mirroring the deliverable format expected from a professional penetration test.

Why this matteredScoring findings with CVSS forces a consistent, defensible severity ranking instead of a gut-feel 'this seems bad' — the same discipline expected in real engagement reports.
